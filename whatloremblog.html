What is the Margaret Thatcher and Liverpool Football Club connection?

1st October, 2021

Liverpool Football Club was founded by John Houlding in 1892 after falling out with Everton football committee. Houdling was the owner of Anfield and club president. Liverpool city, now in decline and rising poverty was an industrial town.

Liverpool the city, with it's port and working class population grew rapidly during the industrial revolution. Most of the factories were unionised. The city iteslf grew at the expense of the exploited workers, many of whom were Irish immigrants who harbored resentment towards the British government. Many people in the city saw themselves closer to Ireland and Scotland than to South of UK and London. The workers at the docks would come to the matches to support the local Liverpool football club. Post world war as the British empire shrank, jobs at the port declined putting many workers out of work.

By 1980s the city was in major decline and was on it's way to become one of the poorest cities' in western Europe. Margaret Thatcher became the Prime Minister of UK in 1979, and would stay in power till 1990 when she resigned after her leadership was challenged by her own Conservative party. She was dubbed 'Iron Lady' for her aggressive and uncompromising political leadership. She was a conservative and a neoliberal.

The city with it's unionised workers had a prevailing belief in socialist ideas. The club at it's peak in the 1960s and 70s had an ideology of sharing and working together and felt previliged to play for the people of Liverpool, almost on the theme of socialism. In 1980 the conservative British government saw this as a threat, and went full out to stop this spread of socialist ideas. When the city needed help, the government under Margaret Thatcher flatly refused to step in and left the city to die.

On 15th April 1989 during an FA cup semi-final match between LFC and Nottingham Forest at the Hillsborough stadium in Sheffield, 97 people died in the human crush due to over crowding. It came to be known as the Hillsborough disaster. To allow more people in, a gate leading to a standing only pen was opened.

In the following weeks, police and politicians selectively leaked and fed false stories to the most popular newspaper in the Britain, The Sun. These false stories ranged from accusing Liverpool fans of hooliganism and being drunk and stealing valuables from the dead at the stadium to a police horse being burned with cigarettes. The Sun newspaper published reports that the club supporters had kicked down the gate and rushed into the already filled stadium.

After 27 years in April 2016 an inquest ruled that the authorities were negligent and the people were killed unlawfully due to their failure. The club supporters and people of Liverpool had always believed that the government wanted to cover up a failure and saw this incident as a way to punish the city.

The UK government under Margaret Thatcher went to war over the uninhabited Falkland Islands against Argentina, refused to support the sanctions against the aparthied regime in South Africa, went on a political war against the miners to reduce the power of trade unions,and actively debated letting the city of Liverpool decline.

In 2013, she died of a stroke at the Ritz Hotel, London, at the age of 87.
